About me

Scientist, modeller, and offshore wind researcher based in the Netherlands.

I am a scientist working on offshore wind energy, with a focus on modelling, simulation, digital tools, operations and maintenance, uncertainty quantification, and environmental impact assessment. My work often sits between technical research and practical decision-making: translating complex engineering methods into usable tools and insights for industry and research partners.

My background includes aerospace engineering, wind energy aerodynamics, aeroacoustics, scientific computing, and probabilistic modelling. I enjoy working with multidisciplinary teams and turning technical ideas into solutions that can support planning, assessment, and real-world offshore energy decisions.

Research

Offshore wind modelling, optimisation, and decision-support tools.

Offshore wind operations & maintenance

Modelling offshore logistics, vessel strategies, reliability assumptions, downtime effects, operational costs, and environmental impacts for wind farm operation and maintenance decisions.

Scientific software & digital tools

Contributing to practical modelling tools that turn research methods into software workflows for analysis, planning, and stakeholder decision-making.

Uncertainty quantification

Applying probabilistic modelling and uncertainty analysis to better understand risk, sensitivity, and robustness in offshore wind systems.

Aerodynamics & aeroacoustics

Background in wind energy aerodynamics and aeroacoustics, combining modelling, simulations, and experimental perspectives.

Selected topics

  • Floating offshore wind installation and logistics
  • Cost and environmental impact assessment
  • Wind farm control and digital decision-support tools
  • Scientific computing and uncertainty quantification
